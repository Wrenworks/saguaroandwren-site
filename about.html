About Saguaro & Wren

The Setting: Sonoran Desert

Saguaro & Wren is set amid the vast Sonoran Desert—a place of stark beauty and surprising life. It’s the only place in the world where the iconic saguaro cactus (Carnegiea gigantea) grows wild :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}. The Sonoran is renowned for its incredible biodiversity and unique bi-seasonal rain patterns :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

These giant saguaros can live up to 200 years, grow 10–40 feet (sometimes even higher), and their root systems stretch wide—up to 100 feet :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}. The landscape is protected in part by Saguaro National Park, which sprawls over both sides of Tucson and safeguards these desert giants :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.

Characters

**Wren** is a small but spirited bird perched atop the desert giants—observant, dryly witty, and always ready with a quip that undercuts the seriousness of the landscape. They see the absurdity in everyday survival, and remind us that even the prickliest places can teach us to find humor in hardship.

Author & Artist

Ricardo Porven is an illustrator, writer, and indie comic creator with a career that spans illustration, writing, photography, and film production :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}. He is best known for the webcomic Donnie Goth—a darkly humorous tale about a teenage Goth boy and his zombie-raising salamander, which gained a massive following online and was even released in book form {{citeturn0search4turn0search11turn0search9}}.

With Saguaro & Wren, Ricardo travels into the heart of the American Southwest—bringing to life the quirky, quiet moments that bloom in arid landscapes.
